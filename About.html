Higherarchy takes a holistic approach to cannabis. Like a farm-to-table restaurant, Higherarchy grows custom strains of cannabis plants used in a propriety line of edibles, prerolls, and body products. No part of the plant goes to waste, and Higherarchy controls the entire process, including farming, harvesting, ingredient selection, and production.

Higherarchy artisanal cannabis products cater to recreational and medicinal consumers alike, specializing in THC and CBD options for all lifestyles. Higherarchy's edible line includes sugar-free, gluten-free, and vegan options, ensuring something for all.

In addition to the specialized line of products, Higherarchy is community-focused and places people and planet before profit. Looking to right injustices of the past, Higherarchy will employ people with cannabis and drug-related convictions and offers apprenticeship opportunities to those who would like to enter the industry. Higherarchy also plans on donating products to communities in need ensuring that price is not a barrier to cannabis medicinals. With a focus on social justice and veteran services, Higherarchy will support various charitable endeavors and set a standard on what a local cannabis business can contribute to the communities they serve.

The planet is also important to Higherarchy. Sustainable packaging, conscious energy usage, and many other sustainability initiatives will be at the forefront of Higherarchy's business model.

Higherarchy is a family-owned business founded by Trimell Hawkins, Eliah Hawkins, Michael Hall, and Jermaine Hall. Higherarchy is also a black and veteran owned business and would like to thank Trimell for his service in the Marines and Michael and Jermaine for their service in the Army.

Higherarchy is a new standard in excellence!





Meet the Team

Trimell Hawkins

Trimell Hawkins is co-founder and lead product developer for Higherarchy. Trimell was born in Chicago but spent most of his childhood in Holland, MI. Chef Trimell discovered his love for cooking when he served in the U.S. military. After leaving the service in 2008, he worked his way through various kitchens until attending and completing his culinary training at the Secchia Institute of Culinary Arts under Master Chefs Campbell, Renusson, and Dunn. After graduation, Chef Trimell spent a summer working with Chef Mark Elliot, the esteemed owner of Elliot's on Linden in North Carolina. He then spent time as Sous Chef under Master Chef Kevin Dunn. Although trained in classical French cooking, Chef Trimell brings a unique, heartfelt style and flair, blending southern elements into dishes and focusing on cooking with great technique.
